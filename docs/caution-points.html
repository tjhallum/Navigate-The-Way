⚠️ Caution Points

Understand key limitations and safeguards for using NTW✝️ wisely and biblically.

NTW✝️ can be a useful tool for Scripture and theology, but wisdom requires clear limits. This page gathers the caution points users should keep in view before relying on any AI-generated answer.

Primary Risks to Remember

LLM limitations

Large language models can generate answers that sound confident while misunderstanding facts, context, or application.

Human-teacher limitations

Even when a tool imitates respected teachers, those teachers remain finite and fallible, and AI can distort their emphases.

Out-of-scope questions

NTW✝️ is strongest around Scripture and theology. Modern events, niche technical issues, or non-theological claims require extra caution and qualified human judgment.

Pastoral limitations

AI does not know your soul, church, family, elders, local circumstances, or conscience before God.

A Simple Discernment Workflow

  1. Ask specific questions. Include the passage, doctrine, audience, or ministry setting you are working with.
  2. Read Scripture directly. Treat the biblical text as primary and the AI response as secondary.
  3. Check the reasoning. Look for missing context, overstatement, or unclear distinctions.
  4. Consult faithful people. Bring serious matters to pastors, elders, teachers, or qualified biblical counselors.
  5. Use with humility. Let the tool sharpen study, not replace prayerful obedience and local-church accountability.

Never Treat AI as Spiritual Authority

NTW✝️ does not replace Scripture, prayer, worship, faithful preaching, pastoral oversight, biblical counseling, Christian fellowship, or membership in a faithful local church.

What to Do with Uncertain Answers

  • Compare with trusted confessions and teachers while remembering that Scripture alone is final.
